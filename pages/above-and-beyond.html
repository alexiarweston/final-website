Above & Beyond

There are many ways to go above and beyond basic cat care. This guide explores harness training techniques, DIY enrichment ideas, and the benefits of creating a catio and/or catwalk for your feline.

Clicker Training

Clicker training is a positive reinforcement method that can be used to teach your cat various tricks and behaviors. It involves using a clicker to mark desired behaviors, followed by a treat. This method can help strengthen the bond between you and your cat while providing mental stimulation. 'Sit', is a great first trick to teach your cat, and it can be done in just a few minutes a day.

Harness Training

Training your cat to walk on a leash can provide them with safe outdoor exploration. Start by getting your cat used to wearing a harness indoors, then gradually introduce the leash. Always supervise your cat while they are outside to ensure their safety. They'll become your little adventure buddy, and you'll both enjoy the fresh air and exercise.

DIY Enrichment

If you're a crafty person, you can create your own cat toys and enrichment activities. Simple items like cardboard boxes, paper bags, and feather wands can provide hours of entertainment. You can also make puzzle feeders to stimulate your cat's mind and encourage natural hunting behaviors.

Cat TV

Setting up a bird feeder or squirrel feeder outside a window can provide your cat with endless entertainment. Cats love watching birds and other wildlife, and this can keep them mentally stimulated. You can also find videos specifically designed for cats that feature birds, fish, or other animals to watch.

Catios

A catio is an enclosed outdoor space that allows your cat to enjoy the outdoors safely. It can be as simple as a window box or as elaborate as a full backyard enclosure. Catios provide a safe environment for your cat to explore, play, and relax while protecting them from predators and traffic.

Catwalks

Catwalks are elevated platforms that allow your cat to explore vertical spaces in your home. They can be installed along walls or in corners, providing your cat with a unique perspective and exercise opportunities. Catwalks can also help reduce stress by giving your cat a place to retreat to when they need some alone time.
